## One-line Summary

This review surveys the first decade of GWAS discovery and frames why moving from association signals to biological mechanisms remains difficult.

## Key Contributions

- Reviews the biological and translational impact of GWAS.
- Explains why large sample sizes and imputation expand GWAS discovery.
- Provides background for the fine-mapping problem GLFM targets.

## Methodology and Architecture

This is a review paper, not a model. It synthesizes GWAS design, discovery, biology, function, and translation.

GWAS is framed as an experimental design for detecting associations between genetic variants and traits in population samples. Power depends on cohort size, allele frequency, effect size, LD between observed genotyped SNPs and unobserved causal variants, genotyping or sequencing coverage, and phenotype heterogeneity. LD is often summarized by \(r^2\), because imperfect tagging increases the sample size required for detection.

The paper distinguishes SNP-array GWAS, imputed GWAS, exome sequencing, and WGS. SNP arrays efficiently assay common variation; imputation uses reference haplotypes to recover unobserved variants; WGS can observe rare variants directly but still needs very large samples unless effects are large.

The review organizes downstream GWAS methods into population-structure correction, summary-statistic locus discovery, SNP heritability estimation, genetic-correlation analysis, pleiotropy analysis, Mendelian randomization, and molecular-trait integration.

## Key Results and Benchmarks

The review emphasizes that GWAS has produced thousands of trait-associated loci but that interpreting causal variants and mechanisms remains a central bottleneck.

Important reported conclusions include: roughly 10,000 strong SNP-trait associations had been reported by the first decade of GWAS; complex traits are highly polygenic; larger sample sizes repeatedly produced new discoveries; pleiotropy is pervasive; common genotyped and imputed SNPs tag a substantial fraction of additive genetic variation; and association discovery does not directly identify the causal variant, target gene, or molecular mechanism.

## Limitations and Future Work

As a review, it does not provide a causal variant inference method. It functions as background for downstream fine-mapping methods.
