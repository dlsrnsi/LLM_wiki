# Reviews

- [[kim-2020-artificial-intelligence-in-drug]] - Review of AI and machine learning across the drug discovery process.

## Comparative Summary

This category currently has one review paper, but it should be treated as the map for the whole `drug-discovery` folder. [[kim-2020-artificial-intelligence-in-drug]] organizes AI drug discovery into target identification, hit identification, ADMET prediction, lead optimization, and drug repositioning.

The other papers fit into that map. [[drug-discovery/drug-target-interactions/lee-2018-identification-of-drug-target]], [[drug-discovery/drug-target-interactions/lee-2019-deepconv-dti-prediction-of-drug]], and [[drug-discovery/drug-target-interactions/lee-2022-sequence-based-prediction-of-protein]] belong mostly to hit identification and DTI prediction. [[drug-discovery/binding-sites-virtual-screening/kang-2022-ai-based-prediction-of-new]] is an applied hit-identification workflow. [[drug-discovery/admet-safety/kim-2022-bayesherg-a-robust-reliable]] belongs to ADMET/safety. [[drug-discovery/peptide-function/lee-2022-amp-bert-prediction-of-antimicrobial]] extends the collection toward peptide-based therapeutic discovery.

## Main Takeaway

The review exposes gaps in the current wiki: there is good coverage of DTI, binding-region prediction, screening, and hERG safety, but little dedicated coverage yet for lead optimization, generative molecule design, drug repositioning, pharmacokinetics, or clinical translation.
